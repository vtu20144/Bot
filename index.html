Winter Agriculture

Winter crops in India, also known as Rabi crops, are cultivated during the winter season, which typically spans from October to March. These crops play a crucial role in sustaining the agricultural productivity of the country. The Rabi season is the second cropping season in India, following the Kharif season, which occurs during the monsoon.
Winter crops are vital for ensuring food security, stabilizing prices, and sustaining the agricultural economy in India. The success of Rabi crops depends on various factors, including timely sowing, adequate irrigation, and favorable weather conditions.

Summer Agriculture

Summer crops in India, also known as Kharif crops, are cultivated during the summer or monsoon season, which typically spans from June to September. These crops are dependent on the southwest monsoon, and their cultivation is essential for sustaining the agricultural economy in the country.
Successful cultivation of Kharif crops is contingent upon the timely arrival and distribution of monsoon rains. Adequate water management, soil fertility, and pest control are essential factors contributing to the productivity of these crops. The diversity of Kharif crops helps ensure food security and economic stability in India.
